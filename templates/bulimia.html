Bulimia Nervosa

Bulimia nervosa is an eating disorder characterized by a cycle of bingeing and compensatory behaviors such as self-induced vomiting designed to undo or compensate for the effects of binge eating.

Symptoms

  • Binge eating, usually in secret
  • Forced vomiting
  • Excessive exercise
  • Use of laxatives, diuretics, or enemas after eating
  • Preoccupation with body shape and weight
  • Fear of gaining weight
  • Frequent fluctuations in weight
  • Damage to the teeth and gums
  • Swelling of the cheeks or jaw area
  • Dry skin and hair, brittle nails
  • Extreme mood swings

When to see a doctor?

If you or someone you know exhibits symptoms of bulimia nervosa, it is important to seek professional medical help. Early treatment is crucial for recovery. Contact a healthcare provider if you notice signs of binge eating, purging behaviors, or obsessive thoughts about weight and dieting.

Causes

The exact cause of bulimia nervosa is not fully understood. Like other eating disorders, it may be caused by a combination of genetic, biological, emotional, behavioral, psychological, and social factors.

Biological

There may be genetic and hormonal factors that influence the development of bulimia nervosa. People with first-degree relatives who have an eating disorder are more likely to develop an eating disorder themselves.

Psychological

Psychological and emotional issues can contribute to the onset of bulimia. People with bulimia may have low self-esteem, perfectionism, impulsive behaviors, and an excessive need for approval.

Environmental

The influence of societal attitudes toward body image, family dynamics, and life events can also play a role. Stressful life changes and trauma are common environmental factors linked to bulimia.

Prevention

While there is no surefire way to prevent bulimia nervosa, these strategies can help reduce the risk:

  • Encourage healthy eating habits and realistic attitudes toward food and body image.
  • Discuss the dangers of dieting and promote balanced nutrition.
  • Foster a positive body image and self-esteem.
  • Encourage open communication and provide a supportive environment.
  • Educate about the impact of media and cultural pressures on body image.
